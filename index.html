Domain Purpose & Intended Outcomes

Domain: Construction cost estimation — the process of predicting total project costs before construction begins, using historical data, material prices, labor rates, and project characteristics.

What It Serves

  • Owners & developers — budget planning, feasibility
  • General contractors — competitive bidding
  • Public agencies — infrastructure project approval
  • Financial institutions — loan underwriting

Intended Outcomes

  • Accurate cost predictions before design completion
  • Regional cost adjustments for location-specific pricing
  • Risk quantification via confidence intervals
  • Explainable outputs for stakeholder trust

Scope & Boundaries

✅ In Scope

  • Conceptual & early-stage cost estimation
  • Building construction (commercial, residential, infrastructure)
  • Regional/geographic price variation across US cities
  • Ensemble ML methods (XGBoost, CatBoost, HistGradient Boosting)
  • Material, labor, and equipment cost components
  • Macroeconomic indicators (PPI, CPI, GDP)
  • Explainability via SHAP

❌ Out of Scope

  • Detailed quantity takeoff (QTO) automation
  • Real-time construction monitoring / schedule estimation
  • International markets (focus is US regional)
  • Deep learning architectures (DNN, transformers) as primary method
  • Construction project management workflows
  • Procurement and supply chain optimization

What Literature Says

  • Source 20 (2020): Regional factor is "repeatedly noted but underexplored"
  • Source 9: Region-specific models outperform national averages
  • Source 8: Incorporating regional economic factors significantly improves accuracy
  • Source 10: Material costs & investment levels are biggest drivers of regional bid deviation

What's Missing

No existing work systematically integrates:

1. Ensemble ML (CatBoost/XGBoost)
2. City-level cost indices (RSMeans CCI)
3. Macroeconomic indicators (PPI/CPI/GDP)
4. Regional categorical features (state, MSA)
5. SHAP explainability at regional level

…into a unified regional prediction framework.

Assumptions & Constraints

Explicit Assumptions

  • RSMeans CCI is a reliable proxy for regional cost differences
  • Historical cost data is representative of future conditions
  • Regional variation is systematic, not purely random
  • Macroeconomic indicators (PPI, GDP) are accessible and timely
  • SHAP values provide meaningful feature attribution for construction domain experts
  • Ensemble methods can capture non-linear regional interactions that linear models miss

Known Constraints

  • RSMeans dataset: 4,477 data points — moderate size
  • 318 cities — some regions have sparse coverage
  • Cost indices update quarterly — temporal lag
  • LLMs show promise but hallucinate on construction-specific tasks
  • No standardized evaluation framework for ML in construction cost estimation
  • Regional definitions vary (city vs. MSA vs. state)

As-Is Summary

The domain: Construction cost estimation is transitioning from traditional statistical methods to ML-driven approaches. Ensemble methods (XGBoost, CatBoost) achieve R² > 0.95 and are the current state of the art.
The gap: Despite strong national-level performance, regional cost variation is systematically undermodeled. Literature repeatedly identifies geography as a significant cost driver, yet no unified framework integrates ensemble ML + regional cost indices + macroeconomic signals + SHAP explainability.
The opportunity: RSMeans CCI covers 318 US cities. FRED PPI provides 75+ years of material price data. CatBoost natively handles categorical variables like state/region. The pieces exist — they haven't been assembled.

Weeks 5–6 — From Gap to Framework
Evaluating Ensemble ML Methods for Regional Construction Cost Estimation

Problem Statement

Problem: Current ML-based construction cost estimation models are trained on national-level datasets and treat geographic location as a flat categorical variable. This produces predictions that systematically underperform in regions with distinct cost structures — such as high-cost coastal metros (NYC, SF) or low-cost rural areas — because the models cannot capture localized economic dynamics.

Why It Matters

  • A 10% estimation error on a $50M project = $5M misallocation
  • Contractors lose bids or lose money on regionally mispriced estimates
  • Public agencies approve budgets based on national averages that don't reflect local reality
  • No existing framework lets estimators understand why regional costs differ (explainability gap)

Root Causes

  • Models treat "state" or "city" as a label, not a feature with economic meaning
  • Regional cost indices (RSMeans CCI) exist but are not integrated into ML pipelines
  • Macroeconomic signals (PPI, GDP) are available nationally but not fused at regional level
  • No systematic study compares regional vs. national ensemble model performance

Hypothesized Solution

Hypothesis: An ensemble ML framework that integrates city-level cost indices (RSMeans CCI), macroeconomic time-series (FRED PPI/CPI), and regional categorical features into a unified CatBoost model will significantly outperform national baseline models on regional cost prediction accuracy, as measured by R², RMSE, and MAPE — with SHAP providing region-level explainability.

H₁: Regional Features

Adding RSMeans CCI as a continuous feature (not just city labels) will improve R² by ≥0.02 over the national baseline model.

Measurable Testable

H₂: Macro Integration

Incorporating PPI and regional GDP signals will reduce MAPE in high-volatility regions (e.g., post-pandemic Sun Belt) by ≥15%.

Measurable Testable

H₃: Explainability

SHAP analysis at the regional level will reveal that material cost index and labor rate index are the top-2 drivers of inter-city cost variation.

Measurable Testable

Data Sources

  • RSMeans CCI — 4,477 data points, 318 US cities, material/labor/equipment sub-indices
  • FRED PPI — Producer Price Index for construction materials (monthly, 1947–present)
  • BEA — Regional GDP by metropolitan statistical area
  • BLS — Consumer Price Index, regional breakdown

Feature Engineering

  • CCI as continuous feature (not one-hot city label)
  • CCI sub-components: material, labor, equipment split
  • PPI lag features (1-month, 3-month, 12-month)
  • Regional GDP growth rate interaction terms
  • MSA population density as proxy for urbanization

Validation Strategy

  • Region-stratified 5-fold CV — each fold preserves regional distribution
  • Temporal holdout — train on pre-2024, test on 2024–2025
  • Leave-one-region-out — generalization test
  • Statistical significance via paired t-test on fold metrics
